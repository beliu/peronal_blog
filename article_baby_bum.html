Can't Go Wrong with Little Baby Bum

Catchy updates of nursery rhymes for your kid to veg out to

By Miranda Liu

Jan 24, 2020

The ennui of sitting through countless hours of kids' "entertainment" is comething all parents must accept. My parents tell me that when I was a wee toddler myself, I would watch this cheaply-produced and simplisitic sing-along show on some channel that is now long gone. According to them, I loved it and they drank wine while waiting for the show to end (hope that "drinking in front of your toddler" part was a joke, mom and dad). That show in essence finds its spiritual successor in Little Baby Bum. Of course when we were kids, all shows were about half-an-hour long and could only be seen on TV at certain hours. Today's kids have the great fortune of being able to watch hours of content, on-demand, on YouTube. What that means is modern-day parents have to not only sit through these shows for much longer, and at anytime of the day, it also means we have to engage in a daily battle to limit our kids' screentime. Tell your parents that this generation of parents do in fact have a harder job than they did, regardless of whatever "modern conveneiences" they argue that we enjoy.

Alright, let's focus on the show. If you are a fully-grown adult, then this show will bore you. It mostly consists of CG animated music videos for classic nurser rhymes such as "Hickery Dickery Dock" and "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star". But obviously, this isn't about you is it? I started to let my 2-year-old watch this before he could even speak a single intelligble syllable. Within a month, he started to sing along to "ABC" and "Shapes Train". This show was teaching him things! I began to enjoy watching the show just to hear him garble out words like square and orange. So a little mind-numbing? Yes. Educational? Definitely. And that's really more important. I will say that it is apparent many parents think the same way because the YouTube channel behind the show has amassed millions of subscribers. That must have opened the cash spigot because sometime two years ago, I noticed a dramatic improvement in animation quality. The characters started to look professionaly done, and not like they were drawn by an art student trying to learn how to use the animation software in order to fit multiple ovals together to form a face. They also extended beyond familiar nursery rhymes into original songs. Most of them are misses, especially the hip-hop inspired ones. However, I must admit that a few of them are quite catchy. One song in particular about connecting the dots uses a punk-rock motif that is an ear-worm, even if you aren't sure what is so punk about connecting dots.

If you have a 2 or 3-year-old that can't follow even simple plotlines yet, then give Little Baby Bum a try. There are quite a few channels on YouTube that do the same thing, but I found the variety of songs on this channel to be the greatest. The singers have nice voices, and you may be surprised to learn that some song channels feature below-average singers. The animation improves over time. Besides all those maybe-technical aspects, the show is charming, with its own recurring cast. It's a totally safe-for-kids channel and it will help your child learn to speak and sing. And that's when you break out your camera and record several dozen gigabytes of video, because that's what modern-day parenting is all about.

Hi, I'm Miranda. I'm a fashion designer for Imomom, a children's apparel firm. I went to school at the San Francisco Academy of Art. When I'm not working on the latest and cutest styles for kids wear, I am dealing with my out-of-control lovely son. Writing about all the shows he watches gives me clarity of mind when deciding which shows are the best for him.
