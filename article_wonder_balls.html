These Musical Spheres Will Have Your Kid Rolling in Laughter

By Miranda Liu

Jan 24, 2020

Silence is golden, and the case of Wonder Balls, it's hilarious. Recall the masters of physical comedy, your Charlie Chaplin's, Jackie Chan's, and Jim Carrey's. They could make you laugh with the infinite flexibility of their body and faces. The characters in Wonder Balls remind me of them, although I suppose the fact that they're animated makes their "gumminess" less impressive than their real-life counterparts. Each character represents a favorite children's toy brought to life. Some are musical in nature, such as a rolling drum and snare, and others are artsy, such as a spherical urchin-like brush that soaks up and sprays out paint. These delightful creatures interact with each other and their world in many creative ways. Whether you appreciate art or not, you'll be quite impressed with the colorful and musical mayhem the gang gets into. But don't worry about whether your kids will understand the humor because all the jokes and comedic action, creative as they are, is suitably low-brow. There is one episode devoted entirely to the drum and snare ball trying to keep in gas while practicing a symphonic number with his fellow instrument pals. Also, the action borrows heavily from the Looney Toons episodes of yore, which holds up even to this day.

The shows animation and action is top-notch. That being said, there isn't much "educational" content. No intelligible words are spoken, so it won't teach your child language skills. There are also no moral lessons or themes. It's just mean to pure eye-candy fun. But honestly, that's OK. Sometimes we should just let our kids enjoy something without having to teach them something. Plus, I noticed that my son started to grab a paint brush after watching the show and attempt to recreate the paintings that the brush toys painted in the episode. If nothing else, this show is great at inspiring art.

Hi, I'm Miranda. I'm a fashion designer for Imomom, a children's apparel firm. I went to school at the San Francisco Academy of Art. When I'm not working on the latest and cutest styles for kids wear, I am dealing with my out-of-control lovely son. Writing about all the shows he watches gives me clarity of mind when deciding which shows are the best for him.
